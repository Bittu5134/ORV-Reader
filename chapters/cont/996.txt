<title>996 Episode 60 Star Fragments (4)
The place where he opened his eyes was a pure white room. A room so white it made his eyes ache, reminiscent of a sterile room. Inside that room, he lay there all alone, like a person who had suddenly come into existence.
<&>「The third Kim Dokja thought. No. There is no such thing as a person who suddenly comes into existence.」
Watching the sentence floating in the air, he realized that it was a sentence describing himself.
<&>「He remembers everything—why—and how he came to be here.」
Three days had already passed since he entered this place.
<&>「During that time, he read only one story.」
The Third knew what story he had read. On the floor where he had slept until now, a book he had been reading lay upside down, its spine exposed.
『Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint』.
He spent his time in this white space reading that story. He slept when he was sleepy and read when he opened his eyes. He was not hungry.
The books stored on the simple bookshelf were classified by language editions from various countries. Of course, since Korean was the only language he could read, he chose the Korean edition.
<&>「Today was the turn to read the final chapter of that book.」
He had many thoughts while devouring the book.
<&>「"Kim Dokja, you only get one chance."」
<&>「"For me, it was always only once."」
Some parts of the book shook his heart, while others made him feel a terrible sadness.
<&>「"Kim Dokja said he read that novel when he was fifteen."」
At times, it was tender, and at other times, he felt a heart-wrenching longing.
<&>「"What if that guy can't read that novel?"」
He also felt an endless longing to see someone.
<&>「"So, you're saying we should show the novel written here to Dokja ahjussi from another worldline? Is my understanding correct?"」
When he finally reached the last chapter, he inadvertently stopped reading.
Why? He felt as though he shouldn't read this story to the very end. It felt as though he wouldn't be able to compose himself if he read all of this. Swept away by a wave of stories that had surged in from somewhere, it felt like he would never be able to rise back to the surface.
<&>「Too much time has passed.」
It struck him once again that he had stayed here for far too long.
<&>「The third Kim Dokja thought. 'My eldest brother must be looking for me.'」
The image of Wukong wandering around searching for him with a serious expression flashed through his mind.
By now, the house must be completely turned upside down. Not just Wukong, but Hyunsung and Heewon must be looking for him too.
<&>「I have to go back.」
After deep contemplation, he put down the book he was reading and looked up.
"Huh? Aren't you going to read any further?"
He wonders when it started. Someone was looking down at him. Pure white skin that resembled this space. A neatly draped white coat.
"That's where the truly touching part begins."
He opened his eyes wide.
He was surprised for two reasons.
One reason was that the man looked remarkably similar to himself, and the other was that his attire resembled that of the protagonist in the novel he had been reading just moments before.
"Who are you?"
He asked, after a moment of hesitation.
Although he had come here, he did not know who the owner of this place was. The man who had brought him here was a completely different person.
<&>「"Friend, the things you have been wondering about all along are written in that story. Go to the place the book told you about."」
The person who had revealed the secrets of 『Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint』 to him. He, too, was a trustworthy person, and for that reason, he had followed his advice without any suspicion. He had lost consciousness while moving to find the location written in the book, and when he woke up, he was here.
"This is my house."
"Your house?"
He looked around slowly before speaking.
"It is a very big house."
"That is exactly the sad part."
He looked intently at the man who said those words. A man who bore a striking resemblance to him. As he gazed quietly into his eyes, he felt as though he were being sucked into the distant reaches of the universe.
"You are..."
"I'm Kim Dokja."
Kim Dokja—that was his own name as well. Yet, for some reason, when he said 'Kim Dokja', Kim Dokja felt as though his name had been stolen. The name 'Kim Dokja' seemed to suit the man before him much better than he did.
"It's the same name as mine."
"Not the same."
Kim Dokja grinned.
"Because I am 'Snowfield Kim Dokja'."
"Snowfield Kim Dokja?"
"The guy I like calls me that. You can call me Snowfield if you prefer."
As if quite pleased with the nickname, Snowfield nodded a few times and asked.
"What should I call you?"
"I."
<&>「The third thought of his name.」
Reading the sentence floating in the air, he decided on his title.
"Please call me Third."
"Alright."
Snowfield and the Third looked at each other for a moment.
The Third was the first to speak. Looking down at the book he had stopped reading, he asked.
"You are the protagonist of this story, aren't you?"
"No, I am someone who wanted to be the protagonist."
The Third couldn't understand what that meant. Snowfield shook his head lightly as if it were no big deal and asked.
"What about you?"
"Me?"
"Didn't you want to be the protagonist while reading?"
The Third pondered for a moment.
Certainly, while reading this story, the Third felt like he had briefly become the protagonist. He cleared the scenario and was with his companions, feeling like he had become Kim Dokja within the story.
"I didn't want to be the protagonist."
"Really? Aren't you resentful?"
Resentful?
"Why should the protagonist be another 'Kim Dokja' instead of me? Don't you feel that way?"
The Third found it difficult to understand those words. So, he gave a different answer.
"I actually prefer watching other people."
"Watch?"
"This story is like that, too."
The Third continued speaking while looking at the folded spine of the book.
"The protagonist is Kim Dokja, but actually, Kim Dokja reads and watches over the other characters."
The Third spoke briefly about the latter part of the story's ending. The protagonist split into countless Kim Dokjas, and the characters struggling. The story of everyone carrying out the scenario to reclaim the protagonist.
"The protagonist gave up his place to make others the protagonists."
As if finding that interpretation interesting, Snowfield nodded slightly.
"But did the protagonist really want to give up his place?"
"Huh?"
"He finally knew the warmth of that position, he barely understood what it means to be loved, so did he really want to step down from it?"
The Third remained silent.
The two Kim Dokjas looked at each other once again. The Third's eyes slowly widened. He asked in a trembling voice,
"You are 'that Kim Dokja'."
Snowfield remained silent. His silence was his affirmation. The Third asked,
"Did the scattered Kim Dokjas return at the end?"
"The end of that book is an open ending."
"Wait a minute, that's a spoiler."
"You would have found out anyway."
Snowfield chuckled and began walking leisurely around.
"I wandered the universe collecting various editions of this book. I was curious if I might see a different ending to this book in another universe."
Only then did the Third understand why so many different editions of the book were shelved on the white bookshelf.
"So, was there an ending you were looking for?"
"No."
The Third felt a distant resonance in that answer. It was a resonance transmitted from a primordial source he was unaware of.
"That is why I felt I had to write it myself."
"You?"
Snowfield did not answer. In that silence, the Third felt an inexplicable threat. This white space, which he had initially perceived as having a slightly peculiar taste, began to feel strange.
"Why did you bring me here?"
"Because I need you to complete the story."
"Because I, too, am one of the countless fragmented Kim Dokjas?"
Snowfield nodded. Watching that nod, the Third recalled the actions of his brothers that he had been unable to understand until now.
His brothers had been trying to protect him from this story. They had been making efforts unknown to him to block all this information and protect him in a safe world.
"In the end, you found me."
"Yes."
"Am I going to die now?"
"No, you are going to read the book."
Saying this, Snowfield picked up the book the third one had stopped reading.
"You are going to finish this book."
"I don't want to read it."
The third one shook his head and said.
"Please send me back. I want to meet my brothers."
"Your brothers are all here."
Saying this, Snowfield took off his coat. His chest was visible through the inside of his black shirt. Sentences etched like tattoos within it were rippling deeply.
"Your brothers—who had to endure a terrible scenario and become sacrifices to dreams while you were hiding here—are all here."
A smell of blood wafted from somewhere. The third one recoiled in fear. But his feet kept sinking deeper and deeper, as if walking on a snowfield.
Snowfield approached the third one.
"You must read the book for everyone. You must finish Kim Dokja's work."
The book he had stopped reading right before his eyes was unfolding its story.
"And imagine the dream we have longed for so much."
The Third struggled desperately to escape. But his gaze was already fixed on the book. Like someone born to read it, his eyes greedily devoured the sentences within. His breathing became ragged, and his heart beat strangely.
<&>「I will write the final chapter for you forever and ever.」
The page turned before his eyes. As the unfolded sentence seeped into his eyeballs, the Third froze. All he could do was read. Read and read again.
And so, he finally reached the final chapter of the story.
<&>「The story she had not yet finished was there. A sentence she had wanted to write someday, if not now. Thinking of that sentence, Han Sooyoung smiled like a fool.」
The moment he read that sentence, the Third shed tears. Deeply accumulated longing and sorrow festered and flowed. Why does the story end here?
Why is an open ending not open to him?
<&>「The universe was opening up before his eyes.」
Dazzling rays of light streamed from the Third's entire body. It was a light so brilliant and beautiful that it was astonishing to think that such a thing had been hidden within him.
<&>「The Third realized what he had to imagine.」
A feeling that the world was creaking and the universe was twisting.
"Next."
The Third asked.
"What will happen?"
The Third understood everything. The reason Snowfield had come to find him, the reason Kim Dokja fragments exist in this world, and the tragedy unfolding across the entire universe as a result.
Snowfield asked.
"Are you curious about the ending?"
The Third hesitated. Depending on how he answered here, the fate of the universe would change.
"Can I see the ending too?"
"It is possible if you help me. Then what happens?"
"The scenario will begin."
Snowfield said with a smile.
"All the Kim Dokjas will gather, and finally, a single dream will be born again."
A single dream.
The Third slowly closed his eyes and imagined a certain scene. As if adding color to that landscape, Snowfield whispered.
"Everyone Kim Dokja loved will gather here."
A thud was heard from somewhere. The Third snapped open his eyes. It was not a sound coming from inside this room.
Thump.
The sound echoed once again.
Snowfield stared somewhere with a strangely hardened expression, then let out a deep, low sigh.
"The person we've been waiting for is coming."
As Snowfield lightly waved his hand, a small screen appeared in the air.
<&>「"Block them! Block them no matter what!"」
At the entrance, which appeared to be on the first floor of the building, people were fighting against men in suits. The first to catch the eye were Wukong and Heewon.
Wukong was recklessly fending off the men in suits while repeatedly calling out someone's name.
<&>「"Youngest!"」
Following them, other figures came into view one by one. There was an old man wearing a Marine Corps cap spitting water from his mouth, an old man scattering seeds here and there, and an old man zapping people with a stun gun.
And at the center of those old men stood a man who bore a striking resemblance to Kim Dokja.
"The protagonist."
The Third blurted those words out without realizing it. Snowfield nodded in response.
"Our brother will arrive soon."
The air shook violently. The space distorted uneasyly, and something was trying to cross over into this place.
Snowfield said with a dazzling smile,
"Let's build a 'big house' for everyone."
